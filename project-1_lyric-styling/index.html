Do not ask your children
to strive for extraordinary lives.
Such striving may seem admirable,
but it is the way of foolishness.
Help them instead to find the wonder
and the marvel of an ordinary life.
Show them the joy of tasting
tomatoes, apples and pears.
Show them how to cry
when pets and people die.
Show them the infinite pleasure
in the touch of a hand.
And make the ordinary come alive for them.
The extraordinary will take care of itself.

William Martin

Everyday is a variable, color font, that features the tools, objects, and imaginary friends that make us company in lonely quarantine times.

This is a specimen an adult life story-book.

It’s midnight. You open the refrigerator. The half-filled ketchup red bottle stands out in the whiteness of the “I’ll do the groceries tomorrow” void. 16 seconds later, you shout in a low-voice:

“ooooh not again!”

KETCHUP GLYPH

Days are so dull. The power went off and all you can do is wait for it to come back.

Actually, you can light some candles!
Exciting!

But in a very old-school way.
Hard to find matches in the dark...

Oh lala.
Here they are.

After nine hours, power is back.
Time for organizing your next week like a responsible grown-up.

So, what day is today again?

Doesn't matter.

Days are just slingshoting themselves, one after another.

Early morning. All you have is the minimum required for a modest breakfast.

Things are bad, but could always be worse.

Just enough for one cup of joe.

*sigh*

A last piece of everything,

and a last scoop of jam.

“Enough is enough!!!”, you tell yourself. You grab your mask, your pocket-sized sanitizer and a touchless credit card.
Of course, you go back to get the car keys that you forgot.

and a last scoop of jam.

There’s the office supplies section. Well, who doesn’t feel artistic during quarantine?

You definitely need some paint to put all that emotion out.

You have to make the most out of this out-of-home time.

Utilities are even more useful when colorful.
(just like fonts)

There’s no possible way you can continue living without all these nice stuff.

Holy crap, you almost forgot the grocecies!

All that you need. Don’t forget anything.

Some stuff to make yourself a nice dinner.

At the cashier now. “They know it”, you think to yourself, “they put the cute things here in purpose for us to buy them with no need”.

You buy it anyway, hoping this time you won’t kill it.

Time to go back home.
You can already feel like you need to shower away all that in-person shopping time out of your body.

Too soon! Yes, dear, you got yourself a flat tire.

Existing is so hard! Now, yes, you are home again.
Weirdest part? Having had to wear real pants.

Adiós, real pants.

In a few months time, will people be as free as in January 2020?

I hope so, you dramatically type into your latest instagram post.

Who knows? Damn, you got so emotional now.
You decide to make some Christmas cards for your friends.

An honorable excuse to put your artistic self out in the world.

...how mundane are things, when put in such perspective.

All these things, big and small.

It’s tough.
You have to be strong to find the ordinary extraordinary.

At times you will fall into pieces. It happens.

At some other times, just watching the time pass is enough.

Slow but steady.

You write all that down, inaugurating the tools you just bought.

Thoughts run smoother on paper.

A tiny thought everyday. It’s been months — almost a full year.

Homeopatical.

Writing about things helps fixing them.

Both in your mind and in your heart.

...you will have enough for a full book by the time this ends.

“Maybe I can even publish it!”, you tell yourself.

Wondering-About-Life time is up!

Hang on.
“Things will be fine”, you think for yourself.
